The 7 Wonders

Discover the Wonders

These are the iconic wonders that captivate visitors from all around the world:

Click on image to open information about it

  1. The Great Wall of China

    2. Walk along the iconic wall, which stretches over 13,000 miles. Our guided tours delve into its historical significance and engineering marvels, making this a memorable experience.

  2. Petra, Jordan

    3. Explore this ancient city carved into rose-red cliffs, known for its stunning architecture and rich history. Discover the secrets of the Nabataeans and the significance of this UNESCO World Heritage Site.

  3. Christ the Redeemer, Brazil

    4. Visit the towering statue overlooking Rio de Janeiro. Enjoy stunning panoramic views of the city and discover the significance of this symbol of Christianity and peace.

  4. Machu Picchu, Peru

    5. Journey to this breathtaking Incan citadel, known for its archaeological significance and dramatic mountain scenery. Experience guided hikes that reveal the rich history and cultural heritage of the Incas.

  5. Chichen Itza, Mexico

    6. Chichen Itza, Mexico Explore the ancient Mayan city, home to the iconic El Castillo pyramid, a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Learn about the fascinating Mayan civilization and the astronomy that shaped their culture.

  6. Roman Colosseum, Italy

    7. Roman Colosseum, Italy Step inside the heart of ancient Rome. Our tours include a visit to the underground chambers and an overview of the Colosseum’s fascinating history as an arena for gladiators and spectacles.

  7. Taj Mahal, India

    8. Taj Mahal, India Visit this exquisite symbol of love, renowned for its stunning beauty and intricate design. Learn about the poignant story behind its creation and explore the surrounding gardens.

  8. Sydney,City in Australia

    9. Sydney is the capital city of the state of New South Wales and the most populous city in Australia. Located on Australia's east coast, the metropolis surrounds Sydney Harbour and extends about 80 km (50 mi) from the Pacific Ocean in the east to the Blue Mountains in the west, and about 80 km (50 mi) from the Ku-ring-gai Chase National Park and the Hawkesbury River in the north and north-west, to the Royal National Park and Macarthur in the south and south-west.

  9. Victoria Falls

    10. The Victoria Falls presents a spectacular sight of awe-inspiring beauty and grandeur on the Zambezi River, forming a natural border between Zambia and Zimbabwe. It was described by the Kololo tribe living in the area as ‘Mosi-oa-Tunya’ – ‘The Smoke that Thunders’ for the immense spray and incredible noise caused by the rushing water. Victoria Falls is now also known as the greatest curtain of falling water in the world when its width and height are combined. Columns of spray can be seen from miles away as, at the height of the rainy season, more than five hundred million cubic metres (over 17 billion cubic feet) of water per minute plummet over the edge, over a width of nearly two kilometres or over a mile, into a gorge over a hundred metres or 300 feet below.

  10. United Kingdom: Buckingham Palace

    11. Buckingham Palace is the British monarchy's administrative headquarters with a total of 775 rooms. Tourists who time their visits right can witness the Changing of the Guard ceremony, and may even catch a glimpse of a member of the royal family.

  11. Greece: the Parthenon

    12. The Parthenon temple, part of the Acropolis in Athens, Greece. Shutterstock Built in the 5th century BCE for the goddess Athena, much of the white marble basic structure has remained.

  12. Spain: La Sagrada Família

    13. La Sagrada Familia in Barcelona, Spain. Luciano Mortula - LGM/Shutterstock Construction on the ornate temple began in 1882 and still isn't finished (the expected completion date is 2026). Over 4.5 million people visit the cathedral each year.

  13. Argentina: La Boca, Buenos Aires

    14. La Boca's colorful buildings and cobblestone streets complete with a lively arts scene make this district one of the most visited (and most photogenic) in Buenos Aires.

  14. New Zealand: Hobbiton

    15. The Hobbiton movie set in Matamata, New Zealand. AaronChoi / iStock Fans of "The Lord of the Rings" can visit Middle Earth in the form of Hobbiton, the movie set used to film scenes in the Shire for the "Lord of the Rings" movies.

  15. Croatia: Dubrovnik Old Town

    16. Dubrovnik Old Town. Shutterstock The Dubrovnik Old Town is one of the most well-preserved medieval cities in the world with enormous walls dating back to the 11th century.

  16. Vietnam: Ba Na Hill mountain resort

    17. The Golden Bridge on Ba Na Hill in Danang, Vietnam. Quang nguyen vinh/Shutterstock Ba Na Hill's attractions include cable cars through the mountains, the Golden Bridge held up by enormous stone hands, and theme park rides.

  17. Morocco: Hassan II Mosque

    18. The Hassan II Mosque in Casablanca, Morocco. saiko3p/Shutterstock The Hasan II Mosque is the largest mosque in Africa and has the tallest minaret in the world at 700 feet tall. It was built in 1993.

  18. United States: Disney World

    19. Disney World is one of the most popular tourist sites in the US with over 17 million visitors every year.

  19. The Netherlands: Anne Frank House

    20. The bookshelf that hid the staircase leading to the Frank family's hideout. Peter Dejong/AP Anne Frank's diary chronicling her life before World War II, her years hiding from the Nazis, and her musings as a young woman became world famous after the war. Visiting the Anne Frank House in Amsterdam where her family hid in a secret annex requires a reservation months in advance.

  20. Italy: Leaning Tower of Pisa

    21. The Leaning Tower of Pisa in Italy. Franco Origlia/Getty Images It's nearly impossible to resist the optical illusion photo-ops that the leaning tower of Pisa provides. Construction on the building began in 1173, and it currently leans about four degrees.

FAQs

Have Questions?

Here are some frequently asked questions to help you plan your adventure:

What is included in the tour price?

Each package includes flights, accommodations, guided tours, and most meals. Please refer to the specific package for details.

How do I book a tour?

You can book online through our website or by contacting our customer service team.

What is the best time to visit these wonders?

Each destination has its peak season. Generally, spring and fall are ideal for most locations. Our team can help you choose the best time for your trip.

Can I customize my itinerary?

Yes! Our private tours allow you to tailor your experience according to your preferences.

What should I pack for my trip?

We recommend comfortable clothing, walking shoes, a hat, sunscreen, and a reusable water bottle. Check the weather for specific destinations before you pack.

Are there age restrictions for tours?

Most tours are suitable for all ages, but certain activities may have specific age requirements. Contact us for more details.
